Biography

Early life in Northern California

On August 12, 1905. Dorothy Chuck Yee Wong was the second child born in the United States (Walnut Grove, CA) for her father WONG Ming Chong and mother JANG Hem. Her father had recently returned from China to bring JANG Hem, pregnant with Dorothy's elder sister Sam Foon WONG. Dorothy's father was a merchant with a partnership in the Chong Sing & Co at the time, located in Walnut Grove, a town on the Sacramento Delta.

The family moved to Watsonville, CA in 1909 so that Dorothy's father could take over the management of then-called Yee Lee Company, changing the name to the Wong Yee Kee Company. Dorothy's three younger brothers and two younger sisters were born.

Sun Yat-sen and Tongmenghui (when??)

Revolutionary Sun Yat-sen is recalled to have visited Dorothy's home when she was at a young age. The family nickname for Sun Yat-sen was "Uncle Bean," which he earned from eating beans when staying at their place. They shared a hometown of Xiangshan, better known by its current name Zhongshan.

Family's return to China

In 1921, at age 16, Dorothy embarked on the with her parents and siblings to return to , China. During this time, Dorothy had also acquired a Kodak Brownie, which she used to photograph her daily life.

Dorothy attended the McTyeire School for Girls, a Christian school in Shanghai. Her parents stayed in Shanghai while she and her siblings eventually returning to the US, starting with Jehim.

She met Chee Chinn CHEND while __ and they married in on June 23, 1925, when Dorothy was 19 years old. They had four children, three sons and a daughter.

Re-migration to the US

By laws at the time, being a woman and having married a non-citizen meant that Dorthy lost her American citizenship. She encountered difficulty when petitioning for her re-naturalization, and had to present evidence that she had previously lived in the US because she did not possess a birth certificate. High school transcripts from Watsonville allowed her to become re-naturalized. She arrived to San Francisco with her oldest two sons Donaldson and Louis on January 24, 1949, and continued living in the Bay Area.

Meanwhile, C. C. and their younger two children remained in China. Dorothy wrote to he husband to urge him to come to the US soon with the impending control of the Chinese Communist Party over China. She vouched for his eligibility in a letter to the California __ [1].

Donaldson and Louis were attending university in 1949, supported by income from Dorothy's imports store, The Temple Bell.
